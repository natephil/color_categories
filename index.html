Color Category Survey

Examining sociological factors' effects on categorization of colors.

Hey there! Welcome to the party! Today you're invited to participate in an interactive experiment inspired by the World Color Survey papers by Terry Regier and Paul Kay in 2005 & 2009.

This is a collection of Munsell Color Values converted into hex values. I collected the color chips from Terry Regier and Paul Kay's World Color Survey. I am interested in adding to the literature on how much weight sociological differences have in color categorization. Questions on language specifically have been seen in Regier and Kay 2005 and 2009. However, a deeper look into other social factors can't be ignored. These other demographics of interest are: gender identity, place of origin, and age.

My hypothesis is that gender identity, native languages, place of origin, and age will all have an effect on the number of color categories as well as color category thresholds. (i.e. I predict women are more likely to have more color categories than men, younger people will have more categories than older people, and finally the categories will reflect the subjects' native languages' natural color categories.)
